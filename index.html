Funnel Diagnosis

Where the pipe narrows

ICP Hypothesis

The growth path: 10-person teams today, 100-person companies next

Today's sweet spot (~10 person eng teams)

  • Engineering-led buying decision; champion is VP Eng or CTO
  • Pain: spreadsheets break as they scale from prototype to production
  • Value prop lands fast — traceability, speed, "deploys in days"
  • Short sales cycle, single stakeholder approval
  • Use case: "we outgrew spreadsheets and need real systems"

Marketing play: product-led content, direct eng persona messaging, case study proof

Growth target (~100 person companies)

  • Buying committee expands: IT, procurement, QA/compliance enter the process
  • Longer cycle, more objection handling needed (security, integrations, ERP)
  • Multi-department value: not just eng, but supply chain, manufacturing ops
  • Compliance and audit requirements become non-negotiable
  • Use case: "we need one system across teams, not tool-per-team chaos"

Marketing play: persona-specific assets, security/compliance collateral, ROI models, multi-stakeholder nurture

Near-term: double down on engineering-led messaging that converts 10-person teams fast. In parallel: build the collateral stack for the 100-person motion so Sales isn't caught empty-handed as deals get bigger.

Appendix — Lead Magnet Draft

The Hardware Traceability Checklist

A mid-funnel offer for buyers not ready to book a demo. Captures email, qualifies intent, and opens a nurture sequence.

12 Questions Before You Outgrow Spreadsheets

  • Can you trace every part back to its source material and supplier?
  • Do you know which build revision is installed in every shipped unit?
  • Could you produce a complete audit trail in under 24 hours?
  • Do engineering changes automatically propagate to the shop floor?
  • Is your BOM the single source of truth — or does everyone have a local copy?
  • Can a new engineer find the latest work instructions without asking someone?

12 Questions (continued)

  • Do you know the real cost of a misbuild or rework event?
  • Are test results linked to the specific serial number tested?
  • Can your supply chain partners access what they need without email chains?
  • Would your process survive a customer audit tomorrow?
  • Are you tracking cycle time per build, or just total lead time?
  • Is your hardware lifecycle data in one system — or five?
Scoring: 0-4 yes = critical gaps. 5-8 = growing pains. 9-12 = ready to scale. Each "no" links to a Boltline capability page.

Appendix — ICP Persona Cards

Three buyers, three entry points, one platform

Engineering Lead

VP/Director of Engineering or Hardware Engineering Manager

  • Pain: Losing control as team scales — revisions, BOMs, and test data scattered
  • Trigger: First misbuild, missed revision, or customer asking "can you prove this?"
  • Wins with: Traceability, speed, no-code workflows
  • Objection: "We're moving too fast to add process"
  • Content: Case studies, technical docs, demo

Operations Manager

Manufacturing Ops, Production Manager, or COO

  • Pain: Shop floor working from outdated instructions; rework and delays
  • Trigger: Costly rework event or failed customer delivery
  • Wins with: Digital work instructions, real-time inventory, collaboration
  • Objection: "Our team won't adopt another tool"
  • Content: ROI calculator, onboarding speed proof, webinar

Quality / Compliance

QA Lead, Compliance Officer, or Regulatory Affairs

  • Pain: Audit prep takes weeks; pulling records from multiple systems
  • Trigger: Upcoming customer audit, AS9100/ISO certification, or FDA submission
  • Wins with: Audit-ready records, compliance reporting, complete history
  • Objection: "Can it meet our regulatory requirements?"
  • Content: Compliance checklist, audit trail demo, vertical one-pager
