Funnel Diagnosis

Where the pipe narrows

ICP Hypothesis

The growth path: 10-person teams today, 100-person companies next

Today's sweet spot (~10 person eng teams)

  • Engineering-led buying decision; champion is VP Eng or CTO
  • Pain: spreadsheets break as they scale from prototype to production
  • Value prop lands fast — traceability, speed, "deploys in days"
  • Short sales cycle, single stakeholder approval
  • Use case: "we outgrew spreadsheets and need real systems"

Marketing play: product-led content, direct eng persona messaging, case study proof

Growth target (~100 person companies)

  • Buying committee expands: IT, procurement, QA/compliance enter the process
  • Longer cycle, more objection handling needed (security, integrations, ERP)
  • Multi-department value: not just eng, but supply chain, manufacturing ops
  • Compliance and audit requirements become non-negotiable
  • Use case: "we need one system across teams, not tool-per-team chaos"

Marketing play: persona-specific assets, security/compliance collateral, ROI models, multi-stakeholder nurture

Near-term: double down on engineering-led messaging that converts 10-person teams fast. In parallel: build the collateral stack for the 100-person motion so Sales isn't caught empty-handed as deals get bigger.

Appendix — Risks

Failure modes and how I'd prevent them

Diffuse ICP

5 verticals at once weakens narrative and dilutes spend.

Mitigation: Tier ICPs. Win one segment loudly before expanding.

Activity without Attribution

Lead volume rises but nobody knows which effort drove pipeline.

Mitigation: Lifecycle definitions + source taxonomy before any campaign launches.

Sales / Marketing Drift

Leads get disputed, feedback loop breaks, trust erodes.

Mitigation: Weekly joint review + shared dashboard + explicit SLA from day one.
